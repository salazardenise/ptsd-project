You are not alone

7.7 million Americans have Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD). It is a mental illness that develops in some people in response to traumatic or life-threatening experiences like war, sexual assault, accidents, or natural disasters. The Finding Peace website offers some resources for people with PTSD. You can seek help by searching for PTSD programs, practice self-care by listening to relaxing recordings, and send message templates by email or text. The purpose of the message templates is to make it easier to send a message regarding your mental health when you are feeling too much anxiety to reach out to others. Have hope. You can and will feel better.

National Resources and Hotlines

There are a lot of other good resources to help people with PTSD and also their loved ones. You can learn more about PTSD from the National Institute of Mental Health, and the American Psychiatric Association.

Hotlines

It can be difficult to pick up the phone and ask for help. Calling a hotline is a free and easy way to get help.

  • Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-TALK (8255)
  • Veterans Crisis Line: 1-800-273-8255, press 1
  • National Sexual Assault Helpline: 800-656-HOPE (4673)
  • Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
